## The 90/90 Rule for Decluttering


One of my favorite techniques is the 90/90 rule: If you haven't used an item in the last 90 days and don't plan to use it in the next 90 days, it's time to let it go. This rule has helped me maintain a clutter-free space while ensuring I keep only what truly adds value to my life. ## The KonMari Method with a Minimalist Twist


While Marie Kondo's method is fantastic, I've adapted it to align more closely with minimalist principles. Instead of asking if something sparks joy, I ask myself three questions:




    - Have I used this in the last six months?

    - Does this serve a specific purpose in my current life?

    - Could someone else make better use of this item?

## The One-In-One-Out Rule


This simple but powerful rule has helped me maintain my minimalist organization system: For every new item that enters my home, one item must leave. This prevents clutter from slowly accumulating and keeps me mindful of new purchases.

## Frequently Asked Questions


### How long does it take to implement a minimalist organization system?

The initial setup typically takes 2-4 weekends, depending on your space and current organization level. However, developing the habits and mindset for maintaining the system usually takes about 2-3 months.



### What should I do with items I'm unsure about keeping?

Create a "maybe" box and store these items for 3-6 months. If you don't use or think about them during this period, it's safe to let them go.



### How can I maintain minimalist organization with a family who doesn't share my vision?

Start by organizing your personal spaces and lead by example. Gradually introduce minimalist concepts through practical benefits rather than forcing change. Focus on shared spaces last, and always respect others' belongings and preferences.



### What's the biggest mistake people make when trying to implement minimalist organization?

The biggest mistake is trying to do everything at once. Successful minimalist organization requires gradual implementation and sustainable habits rather than dramatic overnight changes.
