## 2. The 90/90 Rule: A Game-Changing Approach

One of my favorite techniques is the 90/90 rule. Look at each item and ask yourself: "Have I used this in the last 90 days? Will I use it in the next 90 days?" If the answer is "no" to both questions, it's probably time to let it go. This method is particularly effective for seasonal items and helps maintain a practical perspective on what you actually need.

## 4. The Four-Box Method

I always recommend using the four-box method when sorting through items. Label four boxes or areas as follows:



    - Keep

    - Donate

    - Sell

    - Trash


This system helps make quick decisions and prevents items from ending up in a "maybe" pile that never gets addressed.

## Frequently Asked Questions


### How long should I spend decluttering each day?

Start with 15-20 minutes daily. This is enough time to make progress without feeling overwhelmed. As you build momentum, you can increase the duration based on your comfort level and schedule.



### What should I do with sentimental items that are causing clutter?

Consider photographing sentimental items and keeping digital memories instead of physical objects. For items you want to keep, designate a specific space or memory box with strict size limitations. Remember, the memories associated with these items live in your heart, not in the physical objects.



### How can I prevent clutter from coming back?

Implement the one-in-one-out rule strictly: when you bring in something new, remove something old. Additionally, create designated spaces for everything and maintain regular decluttering sessions. Being mindful of purchases and avoiding impulse buying also helps prevent clutter accumulation.



### What areas should I tackle first when starting to declutter?

Begin with high-traffic areas that affect your daily life the most, such as your bedroom or kitchen. Starting with these spaces provides immediate positive impact and motivation to continue. Avoid beginning with sentimental items or complex spaces like attics, as these can be overwhelming and discourage progress.
